Console overview

See how Ridgeward works.

A walkthrough of the console — from first sign-in to daily use. Each step maps to where you'll find it in the app.

A short video walkthrough is on its way — this tour covers the same ground.
Walkthrough video — drop your embed here
1

Sign in and set up your station

Create your station or accept an invitation. New stations start completely empty — it's your console, not a template full of sample data.

Where · Sign in screen
2

Build your asset register

Add every asset — bridges, pumps, vehicles, fences, buildings — with its condition, location, photos and documents. Each gets a code and keeps its full service history.

Where · Assets
3

Set maintenance plans

Attach preventive schedules and recurring tasks to assets — by time, usage or season. Ridgeward keeps the recommended due date separate from the date you actually commit to, so the plan stays honest.

Where · Maintenance plans
4

Work the jobs, grouped by asset

See what needs attention grouped under each asset. Change a job's status inline, and sort by priority, cost or what's most overdue. Know what needs attention next.

Where · Work due
5

Monitor conditions

Weather, rainfall and wind events are watched against thresholds you set. When conditions cross a line, the right inspection opens automatically — weather conditions may require inspection of exposed assets.

Where · Monitor
6

Work in the field

Field mode is built for a phone in the paddock. Log jobs and inspections, check what's due nearby, and keep records straight whether or not you have signal.

Where · Field mode
7

Track readiness and compliance

Dashboards show on-time work, open issues and readiness by site. Compliance expiries surface before they lapse, so nothing catches you out.

Where · Insights · Cost & compliance
8

Share with your team

Invite the trust, family or contractors as editors or viewers. They accept the invitation, and everyone works from the same live picture. Viewers see everything; only editors can change it.

Where · Data & sharing
